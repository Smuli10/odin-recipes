Pasta Carbonara

Description

Pasta Carbonara is a Roman classic, the secret of which is that there is no mention of cream. The velvety texture of the sauce comes from eggs, cheese and cooking water.

This recipe contains 2 servings.

Ingredients

  • Sphagetti 200g-250g
  • Guanciale 100g-150g
  • 1 Whole egg and 2 yolks
  • Parmesan 1dl
  • Black pepper
  • Salt

Steps

Preparation

  1. Grate the cheese into a fine grater into a bowl
  2. Mix in the egg, egg yolks and plenty of black pepper. Mix until smooth

Cooking pasta

  1. Boil the pasta in salted water until al dente (a little bit firm to the bite)
  • Important: Save about 1–2 dl of pasta cooking water before draining the pasta!

Roasting meat

  1. Cut the meat into small cubes or strips
  2. Fry the meat in a cold pan (without oil) until the fat melts and the meat is crispy
  3. Remove the pan from the stove when the meat is ready

Assembly (Critical step!)

  1. Add the drained pasta to the pan with the meat and fat. Mix well
  2. Pour the egg and cheese mixture into the pasta. The pan must not be too hot or the eggs will turn into an omelette!
  3. Add a splash of the pasta water you saved and stir vigorously. The water and egg-cheese mixture will emulsify in the heat into a perfect, glossy sauce
  4. Add water gradually until the consistency is suitable

And now enjoy it you have deserved it!
